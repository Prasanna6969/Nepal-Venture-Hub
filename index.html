Start Your Startup
From idea to launch in Nepal

Nepal's startup ecosystem is young but growing fast. Here's how to go from problem to product — legally, financially, and strategically.

01
Validate the Problem
Talk to at least 50 potential users before writing code. Use Problem Board above. Go to rural areas — that's where the real problems live. Document every interview.
02
Register Your Company
Register as a Private Limited Company at the Company Registrar's Office, Tripureshwor. Cost: NPR 5,000–20,000. Takes 3–7 days. You'll need PAN/VAT registration too.
03
Build an MVP
Keep it simple. Build for feature phones first — 60% of Nepal still isn't on smartphone. Test in one district before scaling. Purwanchal and Madhesh are great early markets.
04
Access Funding
Apply to SEBON for private equity rules. Reach out to Business Oxygen (BO2), True North Associates, or Dolma Impact Fund. Government's FNCCI and NRB both have SME loan schemes.
05
Understand Regulations
Fintech needs NRB license. Healthcare apps need Dept. of Health approval. Foreign investment follows FITTA 2019. Data laws are still evolving — stay updated via MOICS.
06
Scale Beyond Nepal
Nepal is a testbed for South Asia. What works here often works in Bangladesh, Myanmar, Bhutan. Target diaspora (4M+ abroad) early. Flipside: position Nepal as low-CAC pilot market for global investors.

Resources & Ecosystem
Everything you need to start

From legal registration to investor intros — 12 essential links to Nepal's startup ecosystem, all in one place.

Legal
Company Registrar's Office Nepal
Official government portal to register a Private Limited Company in Nepal. Submit documents, pay fees (NPR 5,000–20,000), and track your application status online.
ocr.gov.np →
Legal
Inland Revenue Department — PAN/VAT
Register for PAN and VAT after company incorporation. Required for all businesses operating in Nepal. Also handles tax filing, TDS, and compliance.
ird.gov.np →
Funding
Dolma Impact Fund
Nepal's leading impact-focused private equity fund. Invests in sectors like healthcare, education, and financial inclusion. Check sizes range from $500K to $5M.
dolmafund.com →
Funding
Business Oxygen (BO2)
Nepal's first angel investment network. Connects early-stage startups with local investors. Apply for funding, mentorship, and access to BO2's founder network.
bo2.com.np →
Funding
ADB Ventures
Asian Development Bank's early-stage investment arm. Funds tech startups solving climate, health, and financial inclusion challenges across Asia, including Nepal.
ventures.adb.org →
Community
Bihe.ai — Nepal's Startup Hub
Nepal's most active startup community and incubator. Hosts demo days, networking events, and an incubation program for early-stage founders. Great for co-founder search.
bihe.ai →
Community
Antarprerana Nepal
Entrepreneurship fellowship and community for Nepali founders. Runs founder programs, workshops, and connects entrepreneurs with mentors and early investors.
antarprerana.com.np →
Regulation
Nepal Rastra Bank — Fintech & Licensing
Central bank of Nepal. Essential for fintech startups — governs payment systems, mobile banking, e-wallets, and remittance licensing. Regulatory sandbox info available here.
nrb.org.np →
Regulation
SEBON — Securities Board of Nepal
Regulates securities markets and private equity in Nepal. Important for startups looking to raise structured equity rounds or issue shares to foreign investors under FITTA 2019.
sebon.gov.np →
